Mobile Action's dashboard is the heart and soul of the company and what it has to offer. This product feed users information and analytics on not only their app performance but also their competitors' performance on the Google Play Store and Apple App Store. All the data provided by Mobile Action analytics can help users become more proactive in making their app gain more visibility and generate more downloads.

The focus and goal of this complete UI redesign was simplify the old design and streamlining the process for users to easily optimize their application.

The design process that was used in this case was the “Double Diamond.”

DISCOVER • RESEARCH

When gathering user research findings, the first step was to understand the users for this current dashboard and their current pain-points. The intention was to uncover how to better design the dashboard for the users that addresses all of their needs. With a more human-centered design that is more intuitive, the team hope increase customer retention and gain more users. For these findings, the following questions were asked.

REGARDING CUSTOMER ISSUES

What issues do most users have when they call in for support?

How often does that question come up?

How does the issue affect their psyche?

How many issues do users have on average? What is the highest number ever from one user?

How similar are the users issues and questions?

How do these issues affect their overall perception of Mobile Action?

REGARDING PRODUCT HISTORY

What is the highest priority product issue that the team currently have to solve?

How was that issue discovered? How are most issues discovered?

Is pushing new features more important or fixing standing issues? How is it decided?

* Most of the research and discovery were done before I had joined the team. However, if given the chance to talk to customers directly, I would interview them through a shared screen where I can watch them use the product.

DEVELOP • IDEATION

Working with the product team to decide on the layout presentation of the new UI, one of the essential focuses was the navigation. For the main navigation on the left side, I wanted to transform the old dropdown accordion list to a more openly transparent navigation list for users to quick access any desired features. Also, moving the fundamental navigation options such as "Settings" to the top right corner of the page allows the focus for the side bar navigation to be solely on app relevance.

CHALLENGES AND SOLUTIONS

One of the main challenges imposed on this project was the time frame and role responsibilities. Agile development was necessary for quick pushes and multiple iterations for production. As a team, we had to repeatedly learn how to not get caught up with the little details and to release quickly to make deadlines. Communication was also another major factor since the Project Managers, whose English was a second language. Also, there was an unclear role assignment on who was the primary Designers for the project.

Problem 1: Project Managers seeking perfection instead of embracing rapid prototyping.

Problem 2: Miscommunication due to language barrier and cultural backgrounds.

Problem 3: Roles were not clearly defined and design ownership was often shifted.
