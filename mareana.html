About the project

Mareana is a big data company that wants to expand into the Human Resources industry with their technology platform services. I collaborated with two Cornell Jonhson MBA students to understand the HR industry and its competitors and how Mareana software can best serve HR professionals.

Mareana core technology, qSuite, provides data mining services with qFind, qArt, and qSci, and qSync. qSync allows users to locate, extract, merge, and publish data from unstructured sources to analyze the best option for its purpose. However, the user interface for qSync lacks an intuitive user experience for its users to quickly adapt to the product.

The technology and design goals and expectations from this client was to uncover insights from HR professionals, identify their pain-points, and design a solution to how qSync's technology could be leveraged to address the users needs.

USER JOURNEY

Susan wants to make a fair wage offer to an applicant based on their experience, education, and other employees in the similar job functions without having to manually pulling data from different reports. With qSync, Susan can quickly search for the applicant's name and find a recommended wage offer suggested by qSync based on Susan's desired metrics and criteria.

RAPID PROTOTYPING

Wireframe

Lo-fi mockups (Sketch)

Hi-fi prototype (InVision)

Keeping the problem definition and user journey in mind, I drew the design and structure for the popup and how it would flow through the interaction. Also, I documented what would most likely be needed on each screens and how it could potentially look.

After drafting the information architect and layouts, I created simple wireframes to demonstrate to the stakeholders the user flow and interactions.

Once the wireframes were approved, I started designing the popup. Initially, I wanted something more bold and colorful so the first iteration had a white texts on a blue gradient background. After some user tests, the results indicated that the colors were well received, but the neutral state of the search box did not stand out and the options did not look clickable.

The final iteration of the popup design had a different background layout for a more visible search bar and a triangle that indicates to user that the options below were clickable.

BRAND STYLE GUIDE

A guide for the popup module and tables within qSync

I based the color scheme off of Mareana's current brand colors and the hues from within their qSuite dashboard. However, there was a lack of consistency of font size and palette colors so I narrowed the guide down to a few main components to give the qSync a more uniformed look and for the developers to reference when they build the popup feature.
